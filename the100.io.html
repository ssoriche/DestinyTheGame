7 October

I wanted to take a moment to talk about experience, raiding, and game postings.

Experience

First of all, an inexperienced player is NOT a bad player. It just means that they aren't familiar with a certain piece of content. We were all inexperienced at one point.

Second, it is also NOT everyone else's job to make sure you get that experience. It is YOUR job to read guides, watch videos, ask questions, etc. The onus is on you.

That said, a lot of us are sherpas and really like introducing content to newer players.

Raiding

This is the content everyone wants to get into. And rightfully so, it's amazing in there!

But before you sign up for a raid, make sure that you are "raid ready". What does that mean?

  1. have the appropriate light level; with the loadout that you plan to run.
  2. use the correct subclass; I know you want to use your new subclass but if it's not leveled, don't...
  3. bring appropriate weapons for each encounter.
  4. research the fights!

Game Postings

These are all posted by you guys, and some of them will have requirements. The requirements will vary from person to person, but I ask that we all respect those requirements when joining a game.

There will be games that require experienced players. This doesn't make that person an asshole. Maybe they don't have time to teach mechanics and want to get through as fast as possible.

There will also be games for those needing a sherpa.

If you're inexperienced, don't sign up for the fast run. Sign up for the sherpa run.
